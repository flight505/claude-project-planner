## Project Overview *

**Project Name**: Task Tracker Pro

**Description**:
A simple web-based task tracking application for small teams. Features include task creation, assignment, status tracking, and basic reporting. Targets small businesses and startups (5-20 employees) who need lightweight project management without the complexity of enterprise tools.

## Target Audience *

**Primary Users**: Small business owners and team leads at startups

**User Personas**:
1. Team Lead: Needs to assign tasks, track progress, and see team workload
2. Team Member: Needs to view assigned tasks, update status, and communicate with teammates

**Geographic Focus**: North America initially

**Market Size**: ~2 million small businesses in target segment

## Key Goals & Success Metrics *

**Primary Objective**:
Help small teams stay organized without overwhelming complexity

**Success Metrics**:
1. 1,000 active users in year 1
2. 99% uptime
3. Average task completion rate increase of 25%

**Business Model**:
Freemium: Free for up to 5 users, $10/user/month for teams

## Technical Requirements *

**Core Features**:
1. Task creation and assignment
2. Status tracking (todo, in progress, done)
3. Team dashboard with task overview
4. Basic notifications via email

**Integrations Required**:
Email service (SendGrid or similar), optional Slack integration

**Data Requirements**:
Users, teams, tasks, comments, task history

**Compliance/Security**:
Basic data encryption, GDPR compliance for EU users

## Constraints & Limitations

**Timeline**:
MVP in 3 months, public beta in 6 months

**Budget**:
$25,000 development budget, $500/month initial operations

**Technical Constraints**:
Must be web-based, mobile-responsive design

**Team**:
1 full-stack developer, 1 part-time designer

**Scalability**:
Start with 100 users, scale to 5,000 in year 1

## Technology Preferences

**Preferred Technology Stack**:
Modern web stack - open to recommendations

**Cloud Provider Preference**:
AWS or similar

**Development Approach**:
Agile, 2-week sprints

**Existing Infrastructure**:
Starting from scratch

## Go-to-Market Strategy

**Launch Strategy**:
Private beta with 20 pilot users, then public launch

**Marketing Channels**:
Product Hunt, Reddit communities, content marketing

**Competition**:
Trello, Asana - we differentiate with simplicity and lower cost

**Pricing Strategy**:
Free tier to attract users, paid plans for growing teams

## Optional: Additional Context

**Problem Statement**:
Small teams find existing project management tools too complex and expensive

**Unique Value Proposition**:
Simple, affordable, and quick to learn - no training required

**Key Assumptions**:
Small teams prefer simplicity over feature richness

**Risks & Concerns**:
Market is crowded, need strong differentiation
